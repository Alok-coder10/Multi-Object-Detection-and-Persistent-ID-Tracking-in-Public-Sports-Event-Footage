YOLOv8 Detection

YOLOv8 is a single-stage anchor-free detector that processes the full image in one forward pass, outputting bounding boxes and class probabilities simultaneously.

Why YOLOv8m?

  • 50.2 mAP on COCO val2017 — strong recall for partially visible players
  • ~20 fps on CPU — viable for offline batch processing
  • Anchor-free head reduces hyperparameter sensitivity
  • Trained on COCO class 0 (person) — ideal for sports footage
  • Trivially swappable to YOLOv8l/x for GPU deployments

ByteTrack tracking loop

Each frame runs a predict → split → assign → update cycle. The two-stage association is the core innovation over classic SORT.

1
Kalman predict
Every active track advances its Kalman state: x̂ = F·x. The 7-dimensional state vector (cx, cy, area, aspect, vx, vy, v_area) predicts where each player will be — before any detections are seen.
2
Split by confidence
Detections split into high-conf (≥0.5) and low-conf (0.1–0.5). This is ByteTrack's key insight — low-conf boxes are often real players that are partially occluded or motion-blurred.
3
Stage 1 — High-conf assignment
IoU cost matrix between predicted boxes and high-conf detections. Hungarian algorithm finds globally optimal matching (threshold 0.30). Matched tracks get a Kalman measurement update.
4
Stage 2 — Recovery pass
Low-conf detections are matched against tracks that were unmatched in Stage 1 (threshold 0.20). This recovers players who temporarily dropped below the confidence threshold.
5
New track creation
Unmatched high-conf detections spawn new KalmanBoxTracker instances with monotonically increasing IDs. A new track requires min_hits=3 consecutive matches before being confirmed.
6
Track pruning
Tracks with time_since_update > max_age (30 frames) are deleted. Their IDs are never reused — ensuring a unique global count of all subjects seen in the video.
